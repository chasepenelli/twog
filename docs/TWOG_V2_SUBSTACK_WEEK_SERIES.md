## Note 1: The Core Concept

The starting point for TWOG v2 is not just "how do we organize more papers?"

It is a more interesting question:

What becomes possible when frontier AI tools, structured evidence pipelines, scientific databases, orchestration systems, and validation workflows are designed as one research stack?

That is the concept behind TWOG v2.

Not a static database.
Not a chatbot.
Not a one-off analysis.

A research operating system.

One that can discover evidence, preserve provenance, use AI for structured synthesis, connect findings to scientific models, move promising ideas into validation planning, and feed results back into memory.

The first focus is comparative oncology, especially canine hemangiosarcoma and related human angiosarcoma research.

The longer-term direction is end-to-end: discovery to modeling to validation to results, with human judgment and source traceability kept in the loop.

---

## Note 2: The New Evidence Layer

The opportunity for TWOG v2 comes from bringing many evidence streams into one structured layer.

For canine hemangiosarcoma and comparative oncology, useful signals can show up in journal abstracts, full-text papers, clinical trial records, veterinary trial registries, molecular databases, compound records, sequencing datasets, safety reports, and sometimes early public discussion.

Frontier tools make it possible to watch more of that landscape, but the important part is not volume alone.

So TWOG v2 is being designed around source lanes.

That means a paper, a trial, a compound record, a dataset, and a social signal are not treated as the same kind of evidence. Each gets its own context and its own level of trust.

This matters because discovery is not proof.

A signal can be interesting without being reliable yet. The system has to preserve that distinction.

It also has to preserve source quality because later modeling and validation work are only as useful as the evidence they are built on.

## Note 3: AI With A Job Description

TWOG v2 uses AI, but I do not think of it as a chatbot.

The goal is not to ask a model for a confident answer and then hope the answer is right.

The better pattern is:

Give the AI a narrow task.
Attach the source trail.
Ask for a structured output.
Record what it did.
Check the result.
Route important decisions through human review.

That is the kind of AI support that feels useful for research.

AI can summarize, compare, draft, help surface missing context, and help turn evidence into clearer hypotheses. But it should not become the authority on what is true, what is clinically meaningful, or what deserves action.

In TWOG v2, AI is meant to help humans aim their judgment, not replace it.

## Note 4: From Interesting To Testable

One of the hardest parts of research is the handoff between "this looks interesting" and "what should we do next?"

Promising ideas can get stuck in notes, meetings, inboxes, or memory.

TWOG v2 tries to make that handoff explicit through a validation queue.

A lead should be able to carry its context with it:

What is being proposed?
What evidence supports it?
What is uncertain?
What kind of validation or modeling might make sense?
Is it ready, blocked, deferred, rejected, or approved?

That is a different posture than simply collecting papers.

It says the system should not only help find ideas. It should help make the next responsible step easier to see.

The long-term goal is to move from "interesting" to "testable" with the source trail still attached.

---

## Note 5: Modeling Needs Memory

The next frontier for TWOG v2 is not only better literature review.

It is moving toward scientific modeling and validation support: connecting pathways, targets, compounds, datasets, and disease biology in ways that can generate better hypotheses.

That kind of modeling needs a strong foundation.

What evidence was used?
Which source lane did it come from?
What assumptions were made?
What model or analysis was run?
What artifact was produced?
Was the result reviewed?

That is where the "boring" pieces matter: ledgers, queues, source labels, approval states, audit trails, and operator dashboards.

Scientific modeling becomes more trustworthy when the system can remember the path from source evidence to computed result.

---

## Note 6: Toward End-To-End Research Infrastructure

The hope for TWOG v2 is careful acceleration.

Not faster in the sloppy sense. Faster because the system can watch more sources, preserve more context, surface better questions, and keep decisions from getting lost.

For a small team working in comparative oncology, that kind of continuity matters.

The direction is end-to-end:

Evidence discovery.
Research memory.
AI-assisted synthesis.
Scientific modeling.
Validation planning.
Approved follow-up.
Result tracking.
New evidence flowing back into the system.

That does not mean the system makes scientific or clinical decisions by itself. It means the path from evidence to action becomes more visible, structured, and reviewable.

It should help answer practical questions:

What changed this week?
Which leads are ready for review?
What evidence supports this idea?
What model or validation step should come next?
What did we reject, and why?
What did the follow-up show?

That is the shape I am interested in: wide discovery, careful memory, conservative action, and continuous learning.

TWOG v2 is not meant to replace human judgment.

It is meant to build better infrastructure around it.

- Repeat the anchor phrase "research memory" across the week so the series coheres.
