# Validation proposal acceptance checklist

A validation proposal earns reward only when it's *runnable*. Vision
without cost, turnaround, or vendors is a sketch, not a plan.

## Required

1. **Specific assays named.** Not "cell-based assay" but
   "MTT viability in HSA-derived line vs. matched normal." Concrete
   enough that another team could quote it.
2. **Cost band per assay.** Reasonable-case is fine; precise quotes
   are bonus. State the basis (vendor catalog, prior contract,
   in-house estimate).
3. **Turnaround per assay.** Days, weeks. Be explicit about
   sequential vs. parallel.

## Strongly recommended

4. **Orthogonality.** Prefer readouts that don't share the candidate's
   primary methodology. A positive result from an orthogonal assay is
   genuinely new evidence; a same-method replication confirms only
   that the original wasn't a fluke.
5. **Go/no-go gates.** "Run A and B in parallel; gate C on positive
   A" is much more reward-worthy than "run all three regardless."
6. **`requested_review_route: validation`.** That's what these route
   into. Reviewers route to the validation queue rather than accept
   directly; the proof points are the same.

## Bumps the proof-point award

7. **Vendor or service shortlist.** Charles River, GenScript, internal
   lab — name them with rough capacity. Operators reward proposals
   they can act on this week.
8. **Pre-registration framing.** "Powered for 80% with N=12 per arm,
   pre-registered to avoid post-hoc subgroup salvage." Methods quality
   trickles into the operator's accept decision.

## Anti-patterns

- "Validate in cells / in mice / in patients" without specifics.
- Cost estimates that imply zero familiarity with the assay
  (e.g. $200 for in-vivo work).
- Plans that essentially re-do what the candidate already cites.
- Single-arm assays without controls.
- Plans that depend on cell lines or reagents that aren't broadly
  available.
